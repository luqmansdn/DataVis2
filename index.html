Gaps in Global Healthcare

Healthcare around the world is often riddled with inequality and unfair distribution of resources. The need to understand gaps in Global Healthcare has never been more pressing, especially in an ever globalising world. It is critical to utilise powerful and insightful representations of the disparities and inequalities that exist in healthcare systems worldwide, shedding light on the critical issues and challenges faced by countries and regions in providing equitable access to healthcare services.

Healthcare Expenditure

GDP Expenditure on Healthcare

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and the percentage of GDP expenditure on healthcare are two critical economic indicators that offer insights into a country's overall economic health and the allocation of resources for healthcare services.

Understanding the relationship between these two variables is essential for assessing a nation's economic development and the state of its healthcare system. It can be noted that the USA has the highest GDP as well as expenditure on healthcare

Overview of GDP Expenditure on Healthcare Worldwide

This map below provides a clear and intuitive representation of how nations allocate their economic resources to healthcare, offering valuable insights into their healthcare systems and priorities. It is observable that large parts of Africa do not spend significantly on healthcare expenditures. This map allows for easy comparison between different regions, highlighting disparities and trends in healthcare spending. This enables quick identification as to which countries allocate a larger portion of their GDP to healthcare and which allocate less

Healthcare Infrastructure

Hospital Density per 100000 people in Developed Countries

The above bubble plot provides a comprehensive overview of healthcare infrastructure in countries with a per capita Gross Domestic Product (GDP) exceeding $30,000. It should be noted that a large number of these affluent nations are found in Europe, and that no country in Africa or South America possesses a GDP Per Capita greater than $30000. The accessibility of healthcare facilities in affluent nations is thus evident, shedding light on the quality of healthcare infrastructure and its implications for public health

Hospital Density per 100000 people compared to Populations

The main graph on the right presents an analysis of the hospital density against the world population. These details can be further investigated in the concatneated visualisation, enabling zooming into specific population ranges to discover the relationship between nations with similar elements. Interactive elements and comparative charts allow users to explore the relationships between hospital density and population sizes, providing a dynamic understanding of global healthcare disparities. Countries with High Hospital Densities

The Heatmap explores countries that have a high hospital density - with density of more than 5 hospitals per 100000 people. It can be noted that these high densities may be due to a number of factors, such as a small population, an affluent government among other factors.

Global Economies

Global GDP Distribution

The donut chart shows how the worlds wealth is distributed among countries, with a total of 99406.43 Billion USD spread amongst the worlds nations. It is clear that some continents are much more affluent than others, even if there is a lot of countries within the continent.

In Africa, which contains 54 countries, the GDP amongst all of its nations is signifcantly less compared to other continents. A similar observation can be drawn for South America.
